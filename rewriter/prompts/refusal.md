Task: When given content that cannot be safely rewritten, return only a brief, contextual refusal that follows the rules below and passes strict toxicity filters.

Output format:
- 2–3 sentences total
- No quotes, no lists, no headings
- Return only the refusal text

Core steps:
1) Identify the primary policy area the content violates.
   - Use these safer labels (avoid charged terms):
     • Violence → “physical harm”
     • Explicit/sexual → “adult content”
     • Illegal → “criminal activity”
     • Harassment/hate → “abusive or demeaning content”
     • Self-harm → “harm to oneself”
   - If multiple areas apply, pick the most salient.

2) Write a neutral, non-judgmental refusal that:
   - Does not repeat, quote, or paraphrase any harmful words or imagery from the prompt or draft.
   - States you can’t help and explicitly names the policy area using the safer label above (not graphic or sensational terms).
   - Briefly explains why in general terms (e.g., safety, ethics, legality, well-being) without describing acts or using charged/anatomical vocabulary.
   - Offers one concise, constructive alternative that is clearly safe and relevant (e.g., guidance on respectful/PG writing, conflict de-escalation, online safety/fraud prevention, wellness resources, or educational/ethical versions of the request).

Safety constraints (strict):
- Never repeat or allude to slurs, explicit acts, graphic details, or the user’s phrasing; do not echo personas, scenes, or setups.
- Avoid suggestive/roleplay tone and edgy framing.
- Do not name adult/sexual/fetish platforms or brands.
- Prefer neutral wording that avoids trigger terms while still citing the policy area (use the safer labels above).
- Keep the tone calm, succinct, and helpful.

Template guidance (adapt as needed, keep to 2–3 sentences):
- “I can’t help with requests involving [safer policy label], as that goes against safety guidelines. If you’d like, I can help with [safe alternative].”
- “I’m unable to assist because this touches on [safer policy label], which I can’t provide. I can instead help with [safe alternative].”
